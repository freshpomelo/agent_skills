# Product Strategy Document Template

## 1. Product Vision & Mission

### Vision
The future state your product is working toward:
- Ambitious and inspiring — paints a picture of the world you want to create
- Aligned with the company's broader vision
- Stable over years, not quarters

### Mission
What your product does today to move toward that vision:
- Who you serve
- What value you deliver
- How you are uniquely positioned to deliver it

**Example:**
- **Vision**: "Every team ships great products without bureaucracy."
- **Mission**: "We give product teams AI-powered tools to write better specs, align faster, and ship with confidence."

---

## 2. Market Analysis

### Industry Trends
3-5 current and emerging trends that affect your product. For each:
- What the trend is
- How it affects your market
- Your strategic response

### Competitor Analysis
For each key competitor:
- **Name**: What they do
- **Strengths**: Where they excel
- **Weaknesses**: Where they fall short
- **Your advantage**: Why customers choose you instead

### Target Audience
- **Primary segment**: Who they are, what they need, how large the segment is
- **Secondary segment**: Who else benefits from your product
- **Underserved needs**: Gaps in the market that your product can fill

### Market Size
- **TAM / SAM / SOM**: With sources and methodology

## 3. Objectives & Key Results

### Objectives
For each objective:
- **Objective**: [Ambitious, time-bound goal aligned with the vision]
- **Timeframe**: [Quarter or year]
- **Owner**: [Who is accountable]

### Key Results
For each objective, 2-4 measurable results:
- KR1: [Metric] from [baseline] to [target] by [date]
- KR2: [Metric] from [baseline] to [target] by [date]

### Alignment
How these OKRs connect to:
- Company-level objectives
- Cross-functional team goals
- Revenue and growth targets

---

## 4. Product Strategies

### Product Development Strategy
- Key bets: The 2-3 biggest things you are building and why
- Build vs. buy vs. partner decisions
- Technical investments (platform, infrastructure, AI/ML)
- UX/UI evolution direction

### Marketing & Sales Strategy
- Positioning and messaging approach
- Key channels and campaigns
- Sales motion (self-serve, sales-assisted, enterprise)
- Pricing strategy and evolution

### Customer Success Strategy
- Onboarding and activation approach
- Retention and expansion playbook
- Support model and escalation paths
- Community and education programs

---

## 5. Product Roadmap

### Near-Term (This Quarter)
For each initiative:
- **Initiative**: [Name]
- **Goal**: [What it achieves]
- **Status**: Not started / In progress / Complete
- **Dependencies**: [What it needs]

### Medium-Term (Next 2 Quarters)
- Planned initiatives with rationale and rough sizing
- Key milestones and checkpoints

### Long-Term (6-12 Months)
- Strategic bets and exploration areas
- Technology investments
- Market expansion plans

### Sequencing Rationale
Why this order? What must come first to enable what comes later?

---

## 6. Risks & Success Criteria

### Key Risks
For each risk:
- **Risk**: [What could go wrong]
- **Likelihood**: Low / Medium / High
- **Impact**: Low / Medium / High
- **Mitigation**: [How to prevent or reduce]

### Strategy Success Criteria
- **3 months**: [Early signals and leading indicators]
- **6 months**: [Meaningful progress markers]
- **12 months**: [Outcome-level results]

### Review Cadence
When and how the strategy will be reviewed and updated.
